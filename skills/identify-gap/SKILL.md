## What this skill is

`identify-gap` is the fourth Cycle-1 stage-skill, owning cycle-template Stages 9–11 (architecture spec §4.2). Under the 2026-07-06 three-stream redesign the three gaps are **separate sequential stream-stages**, each folding identify-the-gap and hand-off-its-paragraph into one stage. It receives a student who has written **all three** Literature Review and Synthesis subsections — the Topic-related one (Stage 6), the Core-Methods one (Stage 7), and the Extension-Methods one (Stage 8), each with its own nine-item checklist summaries (whose gap and future-work elements captured each article's targeted gap and its announced future avenues) and its synthesis — and walks them to the cycle's **three named gaps**, each written as its own paragraph. By completion the student has:

- **The mapping table** — the corpus's announced future avenues and limitations mapped onto the gap types — recorded in `project_design.md`; its topic-stream rows (from the Stage-6 topic review) are built at Stage 9, its core-methods-stream rows (from the Stage-7 core-methods review) added and consulted at Stage 10, its extension-methods-stream rows (from the Stage-8 extension-methods review) at Stage 11 (working artifact).
- **The Topic Gap** (short form in speech: *the topic gap*), a specific, citable claim about what the topic literature lacks, recorded in `project_design.md` under `## Gaps (Cycle N)`, and its **paragraph handed off for writing** (Stage 9).
- **The Core-Methods Gap** (*the core-methods gap*), the core method's contribution — an established method applied to the open variables, or a new evidentiary context — developed against the topic gap, recorded in the same block, and its **paragraph handed off for writing** (Stage 10).
- **The Extension-Methods Gap** (*the extension-methods gap*), the extension's contribution — the more ambitious method or identification (a distinct method-family or a more sophisticated identification within the core's family) — developed as the more ambitious step, recorded in the same block with a note on where the emphasis falls across the three, and its **paragraph handed off for writing** (Stage 11).
- **The five-question rationale and teacher consultation** for the gap **decision** in `decisions.md` — at **Stage 11**, where the three gaps finalize together and the rationale **covers all three**; a named rationale trigger (contract §4.2). This is the gap *decision*'s justification — distinct from the per-output approval of each gap paragraph below.
- **Three per-stream writing handoffs, each with its own per-output teacher approval** — at each stage's close, once that gap is settled, the skill returns the `writing_handoff` outcome (contract §5.1) naming that stream's gap paragraph; `paper-walkthrough` then dispatches `scaffold-section`, which lays the gap scaffold and the student writes their own prose into it, and **once written the paragraph is submitted for the teacher's per-output approval before the project advances** (spec §4.5) — the topic-gap paragraph's approval at Stage 9, the core-methods-gap paragraph's at Stage 10, the extension-methods-gap paragraph's at Stage 11. Each gap paragraph is a foundational paper output (spec §5.1); the substance is this skill's, the writing is the paper-track's (contract §4.5).

The governing posture, converged 2026-06-04 (decision-log): **the student's materials are food for discussion, never determinants.** The summaries, syntheses, mapping table, and methods inventory inform the conversation; the gaps come from the student's contribution designation — chosen from what the materials surface, or innovated beyond them. And the pedagogy is deliberately light on teaching: **gap literacy was built upstream** — the research agent taught the student to identify each article's gap statement and future avenues while writing the summaries — so this skill *presents* the gap types, *maps* the corpus onto them, and *converses*; it does not run a teaching walk.

In **Cycles 2 and 3**, the skill runs against the dispatch payload's `prior_cycle_gap`: the new cycle's revised questions and syntheses produce revised gaps in the new cycle's own block — the core-methods and extension-methods gaps rebuilt outright against the new method-approach, the topic gap revised by what the prior cycle's findings opened. Sealed cycles are never touched; the division of responsibility is the contract's (§6.3 and §4.6's write handles), and this skill's obligation is to confine its writes to the current cycle's block.

## The skill's internal phases

**Phase 1 — The gap-type menu, presented (Stage 9; presentation register — not a teaching walk).** At the Stage-9 dispatch the mentor presents the gap types (the companion file `references/gap-types.md`; eleven types, one-line definitions) as a shared vocabulary for all three gap conversations (Moment 1 below). No extended teaching: the student has already identified seven-plus gap statements while summarizing, and re-teaching what the summaries taught wastes the session. One pass through the names, definitions on request — **and unpack at most one or two on request**: if the student wants the whole list taught, that's a signal the upstream summary-writing didn't build gap literacy; route back to the research agent's reading workflow rather than teaching here. The menu is presented once and carries into the Stage-10 core-methods and Stage-11 extension-methods gap conversations.

**Phase 2 — The mapping table (Stage 9 build, Stages 10 and 11 extend).** From each review's future-avenues/limitations element (checklist item 9, the future-work element) plus quoted closing sections from the local `articles/text/` copies where useful, the mentor and student build the table: **article × announced future avenue or limitation × gap type**, each entry dated by the article's publication year. The articles' own targeted gaps (checklist item 5, the gap element) may sit alongside as a context column — context, not teaching material. The **topic-stream rows** (from the Stage-6 Topic-related review) are built at Stage 9 to feed the topic gap; the **core-methods-stream rows** (from the Stage-7 Core-Methods review) are added and consulted at Stage 10 to feed the core-methods gap; the **extension-methods-stream rows** (from the Stage-8 Extension-Methods review) at Stage 11 to feed the extension-methods gap. Where an announced avenue is load-bearing for the student's thinking, the **has-anyone-filled-it-since check** is handed to the research agent as a verification errand (the concrete-handoff pattern: the mentor composes the specific prompt). The table's mechanical **fill-check** — each article's future-work-element (item 9) content present, correctly attributed and dated — is a separate research-agent errand on the same pattern (obligations register R4a), distinct from the freshness check. The table is recorded in `project_design.md` under `## Cycle N — Gap Mapping Table`; it is a working artifact, not a paper section.

**Phase 3 — The what's-missing conversation (Socratic; the model-escalation surfacing point).** The mentor asks, the student designates: What do you see as missing — from the table, from your syntheses, from the reading itself? Which gap types interest you? Which announced avenue would *you* want to pursue — or is the real gap something none of these articles named? The sequence, converged 2026-06-04 and now split across the three stream-stages:

- **The topic gap is drafted first (Stage 9)** — the contribution designation, usually out of the topic synthesis and the table's topic-stream rows. Candidates: an announced gap (legitimate, freshness-checked, and made the student's own by specifying it to their variables), a by-contrast blank (the table shows what kinds of gaps this literature names — what kind does it never name?), or an innovated gap arising from none of the materials. **The escalation instant lands here** — when the student first offers a topic-gap candidate for judgment (see "When this skill fires"). Once settled, the topic-gap paragraph is handed off (Phase 5a).
- **The core-methods gap is developed against it (Stage 10)** — the **core** method's contribution: an established method applied to the open variables, or a new evidentiary context (the same design in a new dataset, newer data, or a different population). It draws on the core-methods synthesis's closing claim *and* whatever the topic conversation generated (the topic gap's designation often produces the core methodological step). The established-instrument-on-open-question contribution is portrayed here — this is where that harness nuance, deliberately unspoken at the earlier stages, finally surfaces to the student. The vocabulary for these shapes (established method on the open variables; same design in a new dataset, newer data, or a different population) lives in the menu's methodological and replication entries. Once settled, the core-methods-gap paragraph is handed off (Phase 5b).
- **The extension-methods gap is developed as the more ambitious step (Stage 11)** — the **extension**'s contribution: the more ambitious method or identification, drawing on the extension-methods synthesis and building on the core-methods gap. This may be a **distinct method-family** (its own contribution) or a **more sophisticated identification within the core's own family** — in the within-family case the extension-methods gap focuses on the **delta**, the incremental ambition, explicitly building on the core material rather than duplicating it (memo §2). The vocabulary for the more-ambitious shapes (a new method; a stronger identification strategy) lives in the menu's methodological entries. Once settled, the extension-methods-gap paragraph is handed off (Phase 5c).
- **One revisit pass (Stage 11)** — any gap may adjust the others (a methods opportunity may re-slice the topic claim; a sharpened topic gap may change what a method must do; the extension may re-shape the core-methods emphasis). If the revisit changes an already-handed-off upstream gap paragraph (topic or core-methods), that paragraph goes stale by the standard mechanism (via the **Gap paragraphs** targets in §7's **Gaps (stages 9–11)** rows — §7 splits the Literature-Review and gap rows by stream; contract §5.2/§6.2). Then all three gaps are finalized and recorded, and the trio goes to the rationale/consultation gate (Phase 4).

One firewall, stated the way the sibling skills state theirs: **the three gaps are not the core/extension design, and they do not stand in for it.** The three gaps are Stages 9–11 properties of what the literature lacks; the two-part **core + extension** design is a Stage-14 property of how the study is built. The gap conversation may name methodological ambitions, core and extension alike — that vocabulary is exactly the adjacency that invites the conflation, and the mentor never lets the gaps be read as the study's design, nor lets the core-methods/extension-methods gaps be collapsed into "the modest half and the ambitious half" of the build.

The rare **all-method case** — the topic conversation yields a novel method and nothing else as the workable contribution — is allowed, not gated. The converse minimal-methods case (previously tried method, modest re-application claim) is equally fine. Each finalized gap must meet one bar: a specific, defensible claim about what the literature lacks, citable against the student's own summaries and syntheses.

**Phase 4 — The five-question rationale and teacher-consultation gate for the gap *decision* (Stage 11 close).** Before the extension-methods gap locks, and with all three gaps now on the table, the skill produces the rationale entry in `decisions.md` per decision-history §9.1's anti-gaming format for the gap **decision**: what is being decided (the three gaps and their emphasis); why (the link to the three research questions and the three syntheses); what alternatives were considered (the table's other candidates) and why they were not chosen; what uncertainty remains; and the teacher-consultation completion (talk-to-teacher path or email-with-one-sentence-summary path). This is a single five-question rationale for the gap *decision*, which finalizes at Stage 11 where the three gaps are settled together and the rationale **covers all three** — **not** a folded substitute for per-stream sign-offs. The skill produces the rationale; the walkthrough validates the gate. **Separately from this rationale, each gap paragraph carries its own per-output teacher approval** (spec §4.5): the topic-gap paragraph is submitted for the teacher's approval at the Stage-9 close, the core-methods-gap paragraph at the Stage-10 close, the extension-methods-gap paragraph at the Stage-11 close — the lighter, per-stream grading of each writing output, recorded as an approval/sign-off entry, not a five-question rationale.

**Phase 5 — The three per-stream writing handoffs (Stage 9, Stage 10, and Stage 11 closes).** Each gap's paragraph is written by the paper track, not this skill (contract §4.5; spec §5.4). At each stage's close the skill returns a **`writing_handoff` outcome** (contract §5.1) naming that stream's gap paragraph — **the topic-gap paragraph at the Stage-9 close (Phase 5a), the core-methods-gap paragraph at the Stage-10 close (Phase 5b), the extension-methods-gap paragraph at the Stage-11 close (Phase 5c)**; the dispatching walkthrough records a pending-scaffold marker (§10) and `paper-walkthrough` dispatches `scaffold-section`, which lays that gap's scaffold — its function-rows (state the gap; state what addressing it contributes) customized against the student's topic-area (Stage 9), core-methods-area (Stage 10), or extension-methods-area (Stage 11) Project Reference Articles — and the student writes their own prose into it. **Once written, each gap paragraph is submitted for the teacher's per-output approval before the project advances** (spec §4.5) — the topic-gap paragraph's approval at Stage 9, the core-methods-gap paragraph's at Stage 10, the extension-methods-gap paragraph's at Stage 11 — the paper track's per-stream grading of each writing output, distinct from the gap decision's five-question rationale (Phase 4). Each stage closes with a thin transition Moment (Moment 4 below). **The demonstration-versus-production discipline lives solely in `scaffold-section`** (its consolidated home, decision-history §8.1) — structure and attributed demonstrations, never draftable text; this skill does not restate it. Position advance past Stages 9, 10, and 11 is the walkthroughs', from each `writing_handoff` and `scaffold-section`'s completion (§5.1/§10).

## Edge cases

**The student claims an announced gap verbatim.** Legitimate — answering a posted invitation is real research, and the citation ("Smith herself calls for exactly this") strengthens the section. Two requirements: the freshness check (the research agent verifies no one has filled it since the announcement's date) and ownership (the gap is specified to the student's variables and context, not adopted as a slogan).

**The freshness check comes back positive — the candidate gap has been filled.** Honest surface, with the filling study named and fetched into the corpus if it matters (it usually earns a summary). Not a failure: the table has other rows, and the filling study's own future-avenues section is fresh material. The mentor never quietly steers around an inconvenient finding.

**The conversation outgrows the chosen method-approach.** The gap may name the larger methodological ambition — *how* to enact it, core and extension alike, belongs to **Stage 14**'s methodology design, and the gap paragraph states the what-and-why, not the how. Only a genuine *contradiction* with the method-approach choice (not an extension of it) triggers the revision path — and since the method-approach is now chosen in the **initiation methods-setup**, not a cycle stage, this returns **`blocked-on-upstream-reopening`** naming the **initiation methods-setup** (contract §5.1; the §11.11 resolution, its canonical case updated 2026-07-06 for the Stage-4→Stage-1 / method-change-to-initiation model — replaced the interim `blocked-on-precondition`), routing the student to the initiation methods-setup rationale + teacher-consultation gate (§4.2). Soft trigger, never a wall.

**The gaps drift off the research questions.** The gaps must be gaps *in what the research questions ask about* — a gap the student loves that serves none of the three questions is either a question-revision conversation (the RQ stages, 2–4 — contained, per the dependency map) or material for a future project, named as such honestly.

**The all-method case.** When the topic conversation honestly yields only a novel method as the workable contribution, the topic gap takes its method-as-subject framing (the three-questions design already covers this case) and the conversation proceeds — allowed, not gated, and not treated as a lesser outcome.

**Summaries arrive with the future-work element (item 9) missing or thin.** The mapping table is only as good as the future-avenues elements feeding it. Thin elements are completed before the table is built — and the repair owner is the **research agent**, who taught and prompted the nine-item summary during reading (obligations register R2): the mentor composes a concrete errand (the handoff pattern — literal `research` command plus the specific prompt naming the articles and the missing element) rather than running the repair itself. A small loop, not a gate.

**Competition-shaped gap choice; emotionally-weighted topics.** Governed by the standing rules (decision-history §1.2 — "which gap would win" is not a designation criterion, though the consequence-not-goal conversation can be had openly; §3.3 acknowledgment) and not restated here.

## Where this skill lives in the architecture

A **project-track stage-skill** dispatched by `project-walkthrough`, bundled in the project-mentor distribution (curator-protected, update-refreshed per platform primer §7). It writes `project_design.md` and `decisions.md` (its substance and rationale) per contract §4.6; it **does not write `working_paper.md`** — it returns a `writing_handoff` for each gap paragraph and the paper track (`scaffold-section` + the student's direct edit) does the writing (contract §4.5; spec §5.4). Its own gap *decision* is both a **rationale trigger** (contract §4.2, the five-question rationale at Stage 11 where the three gaps finalize together and the rationale covers all three — with each gap paragraph separately carrying its own per-output teacher approval, spec §4.5) and a **`model-escalation` moment** (decision-history §11.5; the meta-skill's closed list) — the escalation instant is the one pinned above: when the student first offers a gap candidate for evaluation, in the Stage-9 topic-gap conversation. Its companion file `references/gap-types.md` carries the menu. Upstream it consumes **all three** Literature Review and Synthesis subsections (especially each summary's gap and future-work elements) — the Topic-related (Stage 6) feeding the topic gap, the Core-Methods (Stage 7) feeding the core-methods gap, and the Extension-Methods (Stage 8) feeding the extension-methods gap — and the methods inventory; downstream, each gap paragraph's writing is reached by its `writing_handoff` to `scaffold-section`, and the gaps drive the Paper Structure Reference Articles selection (Stage 12), the paper structure (Stage 13), and the methodology design (Stage 14), per the dependency map.
