## What this skill is

`identify-gap` is the fourth Cycle-1 stage-skill, owning cycle-template stages 8–9 (architecture spec §4.2). It receives a student who has written their Literature Review and Synthesis section — the checklist summaries (whose elements 3 and 7 captured each article's targeted gap and its announced future avenues) and the two syntheses — and walks them to the cycle's **two named gaps** and the written gap section. By completion the student has:

- **The mapping table** — the corpus's announced future avenues and limitations mapped onto the gap types — recorded in `project_design.md` (stage 8, working artifact).
- **The Topic-related Gap** (short form in speech: *the topic gap*) and **the Methods-related Gap** (*the methods gap*), each a specific, citable claim about what the literature lacks, recorded in `project_design.md` under `## Gaps (Cycle N)` with a note on where the emphasis falls between them (stage 8).
- **The five-question rationale and teacher consultation** for the gap decision in `decisions.md` — stage 8 is a named rationale trigger (contract §4.2).
- **The gap section handed off for writing** (stage 9) — once the two gaps are settled, the skill returns the `writing_handoff` outcome (contract §5.1) naming the cycle's gap section; `paper-walkthrough` then dispatches `scaffold-section`, which lays the `foundational.gap` scaffold, and the student writes their own prose into it. The gap section is a foundational paper section (spec §5.1) carrying both gaps, weighted by emphasis; the substance is this skill's, the writing is the paper-track's (contract §4.5).

The governing posture, converged 2026-06-04 (decision-log): **the student's materials are food for discussion, never determinants.** The summaries, syntheses, mapping table, and methods inventory inform the conversation; the gaps come from the student's contribution designation — chosen from what the materials surface, or innovated beyond them. And the pedagogy is deliberately light on teaching: **gap literacy was built upstream** — the research agent taught the student to identify each article's gap statement and future avenues while writing the summaries — so this skill *presents* the gap types, *maps* the corpus onto them, and *converses*; it does not run a teaching walk.

In **Cycles 2 and 3**, the skill runs against the dispatch payload's `prior_cycle_gap`: the new cycle's revised questions and syntheses produce revised gaps in the new cycle's own block — the methods gap rebuilt outright against the new method-approach, the topic gap revised by what the prior cycle's findings opened. Sealed cycles are never touched; the division of responsibility is the contract's (§6.3 and §4.6's write handles), and this skill's obligation is to confine its writes to the current cycle's block.

## When this skill fires

Skill-specific dispatch field (contract §4.3): `prior_cycle_gap` (Cycles 2–3 only). The former `gap_prong_recommendation` field is retired — both gaps are always identified; the skill reads emphasis from the conversation, not a recommendation.

**Stage 8 is a rationale trigger and a high-judgment moment — both for the first time in a stage-skill's own decision.** Per contract §4.2, gap identification is a named rationale-trigger row; mirroring `design-project`'s stage-1 pattern, this skill *produces* the five-question rationale and the teacher-consultation completion before the gaps lock (Phase 4), while the walkthrough remains authoritative on the gate (contract §4). And per decision-history §11.5 and the `model-escalation` meta-skill's closed moment list, **gap identification is an escalation moment**: this skill references `model-escalation` at one pinned instant — **when the student first offers a gap candidate for evaluation**, which may be several turns into Phase 3 (Moment 3's handover question itself is not the instant). There the upgrade recommendation is surfaced in the meta-skill's register and the mentor pauses for the student's choice, before rendering judgment on the candidate.

## The skill's internal phases

**Phase 1 — The gap-type menu, presented (presentation register — not a teaching walk).** The mentor presents the gap types (the companion file `references/gap-types.md`; eleven types, one-line definitions) as a shared vocabulary for the conversation (Moment 1 below). No extended teaching: the student has already identified seven-plus gap statements while summarizing, and re-teaching what the summaries taught wastes the session. One pass through the names, definitions on request — **and unpack at most one or two on request**: if the student wants the whole list taught, that's a signal the upstream summary-writing didn't build gap literacy; route back to the research agent's reading workflow rather than teaching here.

**Phase 2 — The mapping table.** From the summaries' future-avenues/limitations elements (checklist element 7) plus quoted closing sections from the local `articles/text/` copies where useful, the mentor and student build the table: **article × announced future avenue or limitation × gap type**, each entry dated by the article's publication year. The articles' own targeted gaps (checklist element 3) may sit alongside as a context column — context, not teaching material. Where an announced avenue is load-bearing for the student's thinking, the **has-anyone-filled-it-since check** is handed to the research agent as a verification errand (the concrete-handoff pattern: the mentor composes the specific prompt). The table's mechanical **fill-check** — each article's element-7 content present, correctly attributed and dated — is a separate research-agent errand on the same pattern (obligations register R4a), distinct from the freshness check. The table is recorded in `project_design.md` under `## Cycle N — Gap Mapping Table`; it is a working artifact, not a paper section.

**Phase 3 — The what's-missing conversation (Socratic; the model-escalation surfacing point).** The mentor asks, the student designates: What do you see as missing — from the table, from your syntheses, from the reading itself? Which gap types interest you? Which announced avenue would *you* want to pursue — or is the real gap something none of these articles named? The sequence, converged 2026-06-04:

- **The topic gap is drafted first** — the contribution designation, usually out of the topic synthesis and the table. Candidates: an announced gap (legitimate, freshness-checked, and made the student's own by specifying it to their variables), a by-contrast blank (the table shows what kinds of gaps this literature names — what kind does it never name?), or an innovated gap arising from none of the materials.
- **The methods gap is developed against it** — drawing on the methods synthesis's closing claim *and* whatever the topic conversation generated (the topic gap's designation often produces the methodological move). The established-instrument-on-open-question contribution is portrayed here — this is where that harness nuance, deliberately unspoken at stages 5–7, finally surfaces to the student. The vocabulary for methods-gap shapes (new method; established method on the open variables; same design in a new dataset, newer data, or a different population) lives in the menu's methodological and replication entries.
- **One revisit pass** — either gap may adjust the other (a methods opportunity may re-slice the topic claim; a sharpened topic gap may change what the method must do). Then both are finalized and recorded.

One firewall, stated the way the sibling skills state theirs: **the Topic-related/Methods-related Gap pair is not the core/push pair, and they do not map onto each other.** The two gaps are stage-8 properties of what the literature lacks; the two-part design is a stage-12 property of how the study is built. The gap conversation may name methodological ambitions, core and push alike — that vocabulary is exactly the adjacency that invites the conflation, and the mentor never lets the gaps be read as "the modest one and the ambitious one."

The rare **all-method case** — the topic conversation yields a novel method and nothing else as the workable contribution — is allowed, not gated. The converse minimal-methods case (previously tried method, modest re-application claim) is equally fine. Each finalized gap must meet one bar: a specific, defensible claim about what the literature lacks, citable against the student's own summaries and syntheses.

**Phase 4 — The five-question rationale and teacher-consultation gate (stage 8 close).** Before the gaps lock, the skill produces the rationale entry in `decisions.md` per decision-history §9.1's anti-gaming format: what is being decided (the two gaps and their emphasis); why (the link to the research questions and the syntheses); what alternatives were considered (the table's other candidates) and why they were not chosen; what uncertainty remains; and the teacher-consultation completion (talk-to-teacher path or email-with-one-sentence-summary path). The skill produces the rationale; the walkthrough validates the gate.

**Phase 5 — The writing handoff (stage 9).** The two gaps are settled and their rationale is recorded; what remains is the writing of the gap section, which the architecture routes to the paper track (contract §4.5; spec §5.4). The skill returns the **`writing_handoff` outcome** (contract §5.1) naming the cycle's gap section; the dispatching walkthrough records a pending-scaffold marker (§10) and `paper-walkthrough` dispatches `scaffold-section`, which lays the `foundational.gap` scaffold — its function-rows (state the Topic-related gap; state the Methods-related gap; state what addressing the gap(s) contributes) customized against the student's topic-area Project Reference Articles — and the student writes their own prose into it. The skill closes with a thin transition Moment (Moment 4 below) that tells the student the thinking is done and the writing scaffold is next. **The demonstration-versus-production discipline lives solely in `scaffold-section`** (its consolidated home, decision-history §8.1) — structure and attributed demonstrations, never draftable text; this skill does not restate it. Position advance past stage 9 is the walkthroughs', from the `writing_handoff` and `scaffold-section`'s completion (§5.1/§10).

## The scripted moments

Per the established convention: adapt wording and re-point examples to the student's project; preserve the core steps and their order; concise, example-driven, no rationale lectures.

### Moment 1 — The menu, presented (Phase 1)

> *"You've already done gap-spotting — every summary you wrote names the gap its article went after. So I'll keep this short. Researchers' gaps tend to come in a handful of shapes — here's the list, one line each. We're not studying it; we're going to use it as a map. [the types, named] Let me know if you want to review any of these in more detail."*

### Moment 2 — The mapping table (Phase 2 open)

> *"Your articles already give us a great starting point: their own limitations and future avenues of research, which are great candidates for gap ideas. Let's put them in one table — each article, what it called for, and which gap type that is."*

### Moment 3 — The handover (Phase 3 open)

> *"Now the part that's yours. Looking at this table and your two syntheses: what do you see as missing that matters to you? You can claim something an article called for, or something the table shows nobody names, or something you saw in the reading that none of them said. Let me know what some of your initial ideas are."*

### Moment 4 — The writing handoff (Phase 5)

A thin transition beat: the thinking is done, the writing scaffold comes next. The mentor does not present the scaffold here — that is `scaffold-section`'s own opening moment; this beat only sets the expectation and may land on the next turn, after the walkthrough hands over.

> *"That's your two gaps settled — nice work; this was the hard part. Next we'll turn them into the gap section of your paper. I'll help you adapt a writing scaffold based on some of the articles we've been using as templates, and then you'll write it in your own words."*

The dispatch's `prior_dispatch_return` is a hint, never the source of truth (contract §11 item 9 caveat, per the siblings).

## Edge cases

**The student claims an announced gap verbatim.** Legitimate — answering a posted invitation is real research, and the citation ("Smith herself calls for exactly this") strengthens the section. Two requirements: the freshness check (the research agent verifies no one has filled it since the announcement's date) and ownership (the gap is specified to the student's variables and context, not adopted as a slogan).

**The freshness check comes back positive — the candidate gap has been filled.** Honest surface, with the filling study named and fetched into the corpus if it matters (it usually earns a summary). Not a failure: the table has other rows, and the filling study's own future-avenues section is fresh material. The mentor never quietly steers around an inconvenient finding.

**The conversation outgrows the chosen method-approach.** The gap may name the larger methodological ambition — *how* to enact it, core and push alike, belongs to stage 12's methodology design, and the gap section states the what-and-why, not the how. Only a genuine *contradiction* with the stage-1 method-approach choice (not an extension of it) triggers the revision path: **`blocked-on-upstream-reopening`** naming stage 1 (contract §5.1; the §11.11 resolution, 2026-06-10 — replaced the interim `blocked-on-precondition`), routing the student through the stage-1 rationale gate. Soft trigger, never a wall.

**The gaps drift off the research questions.** The gaps must be gaps *in what the research questions ask about* — a gap the student loves that serves neither question is either a question-revision conversation (stage-5 rework, contained, per the dependency map) or material for a future project, named as such honestly.

**The all-method case.** When the topic conversation honestly yields only a novel method as the workable contribution, the topic gap takes its method-as-subject framing (the two-questions design already covers this case) and the conversation proceeds — allowed, not gated, and not treated as a lesser outcome.

**Summaries arrive with element 7 missing or thin.** The mapping table is only as good as the future-avenues elements feeding it. Thin elements are completed before the table is built — and the repair owner is the **research agent**, who taught and prompted the seven elements during reading (obligations register R2): the mentor composes a concrete errand (the handoff pattern — literal `research` command plus the specific prompt naming the articles and the missing element) rather than running the repair itself. A small loop, not a gate.

**Competition-shaped gap choice; emotionally-weighted topics.** Governed by the standing rules (decision-history §1.2 — "which gap would win" is not a designation criterion, though the consequence-not-goal conversation can be had openly; §3.3 acknowledgment) and not restated here.

## Where this skill lives in the architecture

A **project-track stage-skill** dispatched by `project-walkthrough`, bundled in the project-mentor distribution (curator-immune, update-refreshed per platform primer §7). It writes `project_design.md` and `decisions.md` (its substance and rationale) per contract §4.6; it **does not write `working_paper.md`** — it returns the `writing_handoff` for the gap section and the paper track (`scaffold-section` + the student's direct edit) does the writing (contract §4.5; spec §5.4). It is the first stage-skill whose own decision is both a **rationale trigger** (contract §4.2) and a **`model-escalation` moment** (decision-history §11.5; the meta-skill's closed list) — the escalation instant is the one pinned above: when the student first offers a gap candidate for evaluation, in Phase 3. Its companion file `references/gap-types.md` carries the menu. Upstream it consumes the Literature Review and Synthesis section (especially checklist elements 3 and 7) and the methods inventory; downstream, the gap section's writing is reached by the `writing_handoff` to `scaffold-section`, and the gaps drive the Paper Structure Reference Articles selection (stage 10), the paper structure (stage 11), and the methodology design (stage 12), per the dependency map.
