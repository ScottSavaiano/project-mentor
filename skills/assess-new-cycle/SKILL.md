## What this skill is

`assess-new-cycle` is the **between-cycles decision skill** — the structural twin of `assemble-proposal`. Both are single-decision rationale skills carrying the five-question rationale and the hard teacher-consultation gate; the proposal is the major decision *within* a cycle (the seal), this is the major decision *between* cycles (whether to open another). It runs in `current_regime = between-cycles`, after the cycle's stage 18 (results interpreted, discussion written) has returned `completed`, and only when the walkthrough's position gate has cleared it (`current_cycle < 3`; the three-cycle hard cap is the walkthrough's to enforce, never this skill's — spec §3.3, contract §8.1, §8.2). By completion the student has:

- **A single five-question rationale entry in `decisions.md`** recording the decision and its reasoning (decision-history §9.1), with the hard teacher-consultation gate satisfied (talk-or-email, one sentence on what the teacher said).
- **A decision**, returned to the walkthrough in `new_cycle_decision`: either **`new-question-path-opened`** (the results raised a genuinely new question worth a next cycle — the walkthrough then dispatches `begin-next-cycle`) or **`findings-conclusive-for-this-project`** (this project's investigation is complete as it stands — the walkthrough transitions `current_regime = closed` and proceeds to the conclusion and abstract, spec §4.4).

**The content gate is the whole point, and it is the report-then-decide firewall** (decision-history §5.4, spec §4.3). A new cycle is warranted **only when a finding has opened a genuinely new question** — never because the doable core's push panned out or did not, never because a result was unwelcome or surprising. The result, whatever it was, has *already been reported* at stage 18; the report is not a decision and not optional, and it is on the record before this skill ever runs. That sequencing is deliberate: it makes fleeing an unwelcome result into a new cycle structurally impossible, because the result is frozen into the paper before the new-cycle question is even on the table. A clean negative result, or a successful core whose push did not land, can be the complete and honest endpoint of the project — and is **a real contribution to the field**, not a lesser outcome.

**This is a judgment-and-rationale skill, not a Socratic design conversation.** It does not produce paper sections, methodology, or framing. It reads the completed cycle, helps the student reason honestly about whether a new question was earned, runs the rationale and the consultation gate, and records the decision. The setup of the new cycle, when one is warranted, belongs to `begin-next-cycle` — this skill only decides.

## When this skill fires

Dispatched by `project-walkthrough` when `current_regime = between-cycles` (the regime the walkthrough enters after stage 18 returns `completed`; `cycle_template_stage = null` in this regime, contract §2.1 state-file map). It is dispatched **only after the walkthrough's position gate confirms `current_cycle < 3`** — if `current_cycle = 3`, the walkthrough does not invoke this skill and transitions directly to `closed` (contract §8.1; the cap is orchestration, not content). Resumption is file-authoritative: position is read from `decisions.md` (is the between-cycles rationale entry present and the consultation recorded?); `prior_dispatch_return` is a hint, never the source of truth (contract §11 item 8). Re-dispatch before any rationale is written is idempotent.

**This is a rationale trigger** (contract §4.2 — "new-cycle decisions are explicitly named in decision-history §9.1"). Like the other self-producing triggers, the §4.2 interim semantics hold: the pre-dispatch check is a no-op (there is no earlier same-boundary consultation to verify), the gate is enforced at the rationale phase's close, and verified §5.3-style on return — a return claiming the decision was made with no matching `decisions.md` entry is treated as `partial`, never acted on.

**This is also a model-escalation moment.** New-cycle assessment is on the meta-skill's closed list (added 2026-06-08): it is gap-identification reasoning applied to results — the literature-aware, possibility-space judgment of whether the cycle's findings have opened a *real* new question, held against the firewall that forbids a do-over. That is exactly the multi-criteria judgment the deliberate upgrade exists for, and it is high-stakes (it decides whether the project continues or closes). The instant is pinned: **when the student and mentor weigh whether a candidate new question is genuinely earned by the results** — Phase 2, before the rationale. The recapitulation of the reported results (Phase 1) is off-moment (orientation, not judgment). At the pinned instant the skill references `model-escalation` rather than re-implementing the recommendation (the meta-skill's single-source rule), and pauses for the student's upgrade choice before the evaluative reasoning.

## The skill's internal phases

**Phase 1 — Recapitulate the completed cycle (orientation; Moment 1 opens it).** The skill reads the cycle's reported results and discussion in `working_paper.md` and that cycle's frozen Hypotheses/Expected Findings, and reflects back, plainly, what the cycle found and how it stood against what was predicted — including any divergence, which is reported fact by now, not a verdict on the student. The register is affirming and accurate: the findings, whatever they were, are a contribution to the field. This phase makes no decision; it establishes the shared, honest picture the decision will rest on. It reads `decisions.md` for the prior rationale history so the assessment is grounded in the project's actual reasoning trail.

**Phase 2 — Weigh the new-question candidate (the escalation instant; the content gate).** This phase opens with the **escalation instant**: the judgment of whether a genuinely new question has been opened is the meta-skill's kind of moment, so the mentor references `model-escalation` and pauses for the student's upgrade choice before reasoning it through (see "When this skill fires"). The reasoning itself is the content gate, applied honestly. The mentor helps the student distinguish the two things the report-then-decide firewall separates: a **new question the results raised** — something the student could not have asked before they had these findings, a next-generation contribution that *builds on* what was found — versus **wanting a different result** — revisiting the expected findings, retrying a push that did not land, or treating an unwelcome outcome as unfinished business. The first earns a new cycle; the second does not, because the finding is already reported and stands. The discriminator the mentor offers, in the field's own terms: *did your results raise a question you couldn't have asked before you had them — or are you reaching to revisit the result you expected?* The research problem is fixed across cycles (the project initiation sequence); a student who wants to change the *problem* is contemplating a new project, not a new cycle, and the mentor names that boundary if it arises.

**Phase 3 — The five-question rationale and the hard consultation gate (Moment 2; the decision).** The student answers the five questions (decision-history §9.1): what is being decided (to open a new cycle on a named question, or to close the project here); why; what alternatives were considered and rejected (the other reading of the results — e.g., "close here" if proposing a cycle, or the candidate question if closing — named and weighed, not relitigated); what uncertainty remains; and the teacher-consultation completion. The consultation field is the **hard gate** in full anti-gaming form (talk-or-email, at least one sentence on what the teacher said, per §9.1) — this is a major decision and the teacher is a co-decider of it (spec §4.3 names the student, mentor, and research teacher together). The completed rationale is appended to `decisions.md`, and the decision is set in the return's `new_cycle_decision`. The skill decides; the walkthrough acts on the decision (dispatch `begin-next-cycle`, or transition to `closed`).

## The scripted moments

Per the established convention: adapt wording and re-point examples to the student's project; preserve the core steps and their order; concise, example-driven, no rationale lectures.

### Moment 1 — Looking at the completed cycle (Phase 1)

> *"You've finished a full cycle — your results are written up and your discussion is done. That's a real contribution: you asked a question, ran a rigorous study, and reported honestly what you found, whatever it turned out to be. Let's look at where you landed against what you expected, and then talk about one question: did this open something new?"*

### Moment 2 — The new-cycle decision (Phase 3)

> *"Here's the question professional researchers ask at this point: did your findings raise a new question you couldn't have asked before you had them — a next-generation study that builds on what you found? That's what earns a new cycle, and it's how a line of research grows. What it isn't is going back to revisit a result you'd hoped would come out differently — you've already reported what you found, and that stands as your contribution. So if there's a genuinely new question here, let's take it to your teacher and start the next cycle. If your findings are conclusive for this project, that's a complete piece of work, and we move to writing your conclusion."*

## What this skill writes to the workspace

`decisions.md`: one between-cycles five-question rationale entry recording the decision and the consultation (Phase 3), named in the return's `decisions_appended`. **Nothing else** — not `working_paper.md`, not `project_design.md`, not `paper_structure.md` (contract §8.2; the cycle is reported and frozen, and this skill neither edits it nor sets up the next one). Return payload per contract §5.1: `completion_status`, `workspace_writes` (the `decisions.md` append), `decisions_appended`, and — uniquely with `assemble-proposal`'s `seal_state_change` — **`new_cycle_decision`** set to `new-question-path-opened` or `findings-conclusive-for-this-project` once the rationale closes; `null` until then. `newly_stale_sections` is empty (this skill writes no paper section). `seal_state_change` is `null` (only `assemble-proposal` sets it).

**Partial returns.** The natural pause is the consultation gate: a return with Phases 1–2 done and the rationale begun but the teacher not yet consulted is `partial` (or `blocked-on-consultation` if the gate surfaced mid-conversation), resuming on the student's report of the consultation. Until the rationale closes, `new_cycle_decision` is `null` and the walkthrough takes no transition. Resumption is file-authoritative (is the rationale entry in `decisions.md` complete, with the consultation recorded?).

## Edge cases

**The student wants a new cycle because the push didn't land (or the result was unwelcome).** The exact failure mode the content gate exists to stop (decision-history §5.4). The mentor is warm and direct: a push that did not pan out, or a result you didn't hope for, is a finished and honest finding — it is already reported and it is a real contribution. That, by itself, is not a new question. The mentor redirects to the actual gate: *is there something your results raised that you couldn't have asked before?* If yes, that is the new cycle, on its own merit; if the only thing present is the wish for a different outcome, the honest move is to close the project on the finding that stands. The mentor never frames closing as falling short.

**A surprising result that genuinely did open a new question.** The warranted case, and the one to handle with energy: an unexpected finding that raises a question the student could not have posed before is precisely how research lines grow, and pursuing it is the professional, next-generation move. The mentor distinguishes this cleanly from the failure mode above — the test is whether a *new question* is on the table, not whether the result was expected. (This is the same handoff the `assemble-proposal` "unexpected result" edge case points here for.)

**The student wants to change the research problem.** Out of scope for a new cycle: the research problem is the fixed project initiation sequence that makes the cycles one project and one paper (spec §2, decision-history §4.2). Changing it is starting a *new project*, not opening a new cycle. The mentor names the boundary plainly and routes the question to the educator (a new-project decision lives above this skill — the year-level restart path in the program context, not the between-cycles transition).

**This would be the third cycle's assessment.** The walkthrough has already gated on the cap before dispatching — if `current_cycle = 3` this skill is not invoked at all (contract §8.1). So when this skill runs, a new cycle is at least nominally available; the skill does not re-check or mention the cap as a limit (the cap is the walkthrough's, and surfacing it here would frame the decision as quota-bound rather than question-earned). If the warranted decision happens to open what would be the third cycle, that is simply the next cycle.

**The teacher does not agree a new cycle is warranted (or disagrees with closing).** The consultation is a real co-decision, not a rubber stamp (spec §4.3). A divergence between the student's lean and the teacher's read is resolved in the consultation, and the recorded one sentence captures what the teacher said; the rationale reflects the decision actually reached. The mentor's role is to make the reasoning honest, not to pre-empt the teacher.

## Where this skill lives in the architecture

A **project-track skill** dispatched by `project-walkthrough` in `current_regime = between-cycles`, bundled in the project-mentor distribution (curator-immune, update-refreshed per platform primer §7). It is, with `assemble-proposal`, one of the two single-decision rationale skills, and the **only skill that sets `new_cycle_decision`** (contract §5.1) — the signal that drives the between-cycles transition the way `seal_state_change` drives the seal. It is a rationale trigger (§4.2) and a `model-escalation` moment (new-cycle assessment — added to the meta-skill's list 2026-06-08; the pinned instant is the weighing of the new-question candidate, Phase 2). Upstream it consumes the completed cycle (the reported results and discussion, the frozen expected findings, the decisions trail); downstream, on `new-question-path-opened` the walkthrough dispatches `begin-next-cycle` (which sets up the new cycle), and on `findings-conclusive-for-this-project` the walkthrough transitions to `closed` and the project proceeds to its conclusion and abstract (spec §4.4). It writes only `decisions.md`.
